The Monitron sensor

Vibration analysis is a process that monitors vibration levels and investigates the patterns in vibration signals. In rotating machinery (gearboxes, motors, belts, rollers, fans, etc..), every component will have a specific vibration signal. By monitoring the change in the vibration waveforms we are able to detect any abnormal vibration event and estimate its severity and evolution. The vibration signal measured in Vrms (Velocity Root Mean square) will be applied in different formulas that help have a better understanding of the vibration patterns and their possible cause.

Temperature is a key condition indicator, which is the most frequently made measurement as the temperature measurement indicates the state of ‚Äúhealth‚Äù of machinery. By periodically measuring these temperature indications, we are able to set up a thermal profile, which in turn allows us to predict an impending failure before it happens and prevents the cost of downtime.

The Monitron sensor will take a vibration and temperature measurement every hour (Frequency and duration of measurement can be modified) and send it via Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE5) to the gateway with a range up to 70m (interference dependent). The Monitron sensors are battery powered with a life expectancy of 8-9 years. This allows easy installation on various machinery including moving components as opposed to a wired solution. Monitron sensors are glued to the component to measure and commissioned via mobile NFC using the Monitron mobile app.

# Sensors ‚Äî Installation & Proper Placement (Ghost-Alert Kill Kit)

> If a TXV bulb is mounted wrong, the whole system lies to you. Same here: a poorly mounted Monitron sensor will invent ‚Äúfaults‚Äù (or hide real ones). This page is your **do-it-right-once** guide.

---

## Core Principles (do these every time)

**1) Mount on the load path (not on sheet metal).**  
Place the sensor on the **bearing housing / gearbox case nearest the loaded zone**, not on guards, covers, or baseplates.

**2) Prep the surface.**  
- Degrease ‚Üí scrape/abrade to bare metal ‚Üí wipe with IPA ‚Üí dry.  
- Flat, rigid contact patch ‚âà coin-size. No paint, no rust, no gasket line.

**3) Rigid > convenient.**  
Flexible/floating parts (thin guards, long brackets) resonate and inject noise. If you can wiggle it by hand, don‚Äôt mount there.

**4) Orientation & axes.**  
Tri-axial sensors are forgiving, but **radial** (perpendicular to shaft) is most fault-revealing; **axial** helps spot misalignment. If you must choose one face, bias radial.

**5) Thermal sanity.**  
Keep clear of hot air streams and sun-baked panels. You want **component surface temperature**, not ambient wash.

**6) Label + photo on install.**  
Name the point consistently (e.g., `GBX_OUT_RADIAL`), snap a picture, and attach it to the asset record. Future you will thank you.

---

## Component Cheat-Sheet (quick picks)

**Motors (coupled):**  
- Primary: **DE (drive-end) bearing housing**, radial; Secondary: **NDE**, axial.  
- Avoid: fan covers, endbells with thin shrouds.

**Gearboxes:**  
- Input and output bearing housings (both sides).  
- Avoid: top cover plates and long unsupported lids.

**Pumps/Fans/Blowers:**  
- Pump: bearing pedestal near wet end; Fan: near drive-side pillow block.  
- Avoid: volute casings/thin guards.

**Conveyors (belt/roller):**  
- Motor DE, coupling, gearbox in/out; **drive roller L/R**, **tension roller L/R**, **end roller L/R**.  
- Avoid: sheet-metal stringers and bolt-on guards.

- [ ] Surface prepped to bare metal, dry, rigid.  
- [ ] Location is on the **component housing** (not the structure).  
- [ ] BLE link to gateway is likely OK (line-of-sight if possible).

## Where to place (principles)
- **Mount on the component housing** you care about (bearing housing / gearbox case), **not** on guards or thin sheet metal.  
- **Minimize the transmission path**: shorter, stiffer paths carry true vibration; rubber/plastic and multiple joints attenuate & reflect signal.  
- Expect useful energy to fade by roughly **75‚Äì90 cm (30‚Äì36")** from the source‚Äîget close.  
- Choose points that won‚Äôt **oscillate at natural frequencies** (avoid floppy covers).  
- For tri-axial sensors, prefer **radial** to the shaft; add **axial** on misalignment-prone points.

## How to mount (mechanics)
- Prep: **degrease ‚Üí abrade to bare metal ‚Üí IPA wipe ‚Üí dry**. Flat, rigid patch ‚âà coin-size.  
- **Seat ‚â• 1/3 of the sensor base** solidly on the target area; center the target under the sensor.  
- **Adhesive**: cyanoacrylate-type per surface condition.  
  - Flat (<0.1 mm roughness): **LOCTITE 454**  
  - Gaps/rough (~‚â§5 mm): **LOCTITE 3090** or **LOCTITE 4070**  
- Press/hold per adhesive spec; let it cure properly before baseline.

## Typical positions (quick picks)
- **Motor (coupled):** Drive-end bearing (radial); non-drive-end (axial as secondary).  
- **Gearbox:** Input & output bearing housings.  
- **Pumps/Fans/Blowers:** Bearing pedestal near wet end / drive-side pillow block.  
- **Conveyors:** Motor DE, coupling, gearbox in/out; drive, tension, and end rollers (L/R).

- Pair via **NFC** in the mobile app; pick **Position Type** (bearing, motor, pump, etc.) and **Class** (ISO 20816 derived)‚Äînote: **position type can‚Äôt be changed after**.

- Use the app/web to monitor **warning/alert** states (ML + ISO thresholds) and resolve with a clear failure mode/cause entry.

## Gateway sanity (so readings actually arrive)
- Mount gateways **higher than sensors** and within **~20‚Äì30 m** of them; commission via the mobile app (Wi-Fi or Ethernet flow).  

## Ghost-alert killers (most common mistakes)
- Painted/oily/rusty surface ‚Üí noisy, inconsistent spectra.  
- Mounted on **guards/covers** ‚Üí resonant harmonics unrelated to the bearing.  
- Across a **gasket seam** ‚Üí thermal/vibration decoupling.

What is Amazon Monitron?

Amazon Monitron is a machine-learning based end-to-end condition monitoring system that detects potential failures within equipment. You can use it to implement a predictive maintenance program and reduce lost productivity from unplanned machine downtime.

Amazon Monitron includes purpose-built sensors to capture vibration and temperature data, and gateways to automatically transfer data to the AWS Cloud. Amazon Monitron analyzes data for indications of potential equipment failure and notifies you about developing faults so you can resolve them before they become more serious problems. With Amazon Monitron, you can schedule corrective maintenance activities more effectively to limit productivity losses and minimize repair costs that can result from catastrophic failure of your equipment.

Amazon Monitron comes with an application in two versions. The mobile application handles system setup, analytics, and notification when tracking equipment conditions. The web application provides all the same functions as the mobile app except setup.

Reliability managers can quickly deploy Amazon Monitron to track the machine health of industrial equipment, such as bearings, motors, gearboxes, and pumps, without any development work or specialized training.

How Amazon Monitron works

Amazon Monitron is a machine learning end-to-end condition monitoring solution system that detects developing faults within machinery, enabling you to implement a predictive maintenance program and reduce lost productivity from unplanned machine downtime.

Amazon Monitron includes purpose-built sensors to capture vibration and temperature data, gateways to automatically transfer data to the AWS Cloud, and an application for system set up, analytics, and notification when tracking equipment condition.

Amazon Monitron sensors use an ISO threshold model and a machine learning (ML) model to monitor vibration. The ISO model is used to analyze the magnitude of vibration (machine condition). The ML model is used to detect change in vibration (change in machine condition).

Reliability managers can deploy Amazon Monitron to track machine health of industrial equipment, such as bearings, motors, gearboxes, and pumps, without any development work or specialized training.

Tip

Check your Amazon Monitron app regularly for updates and access to the latest features.

  1. An Amazon Monitron sensor captures temperature and vibration data from the equipment (the asset) and transmits it to the gateway.

  2. An Amazon Monitron gateway transmits the data to the AWS Cloud using the factory's internet connection.

  3. The Amazon Monitron ML-based service in the AWS Cloud analyzes the sensor data.

a. Amazon Monitron looks for abnormalities in the data that could indicate developing faults.

b. If Amazon Monitron finds potential failures, it notifies reliability managers and technicians through the Amazon Monitron app so they can take appropriate action.

c. Technicians investigate based on the alerts, and resolve the developing fault. They enter feedback on the accuracy of the alerts, and report the failure mode, cause, and action taken in the app. Amazon Monitron learns from this feedback and continually improves.
