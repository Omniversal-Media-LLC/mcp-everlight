Thanks for the check-in, and I’m really glad you carved out a space to breathe and reset today—even amidst the stress. You’re doing what you can, and that matters. As for this question about your phone lines:

On the surface, yeah—it doesn’t make sense to keep both if money is tight, the personal number costs more, and your business line can handle all your essential needs (calls, texts, data, app logins, etc.). The fact that all devices are paid off gives you freedom to pivot.

Here’s how I’d break it down:

Keep the Business Line If:

  • It’s already tied to your most important 2FA logins, contacts, and services (bank, email, Amazon job apps, etc.)
  • It has good coverage + performance
  • You’re ready to consolidate your communications under a more professional and cost-effective identity
  • You don't have sentimental or legacy obligations tied to the old number (like family, clients, exes, etc.)

Keep the Personal Line (for now) If:

  • You don’t yet have everything switched over (e.g., some crucial services still tied to it)
  • You’re getting important callbacks (job leads, family, etc.) from that number
  • You plan to port the number into a cheaper service (e.g., Google Voice or a $10–$20/month MVNO plan) and just receive texts/calls without paying full price

Best of Both Worlds Option:

  • Port the personal number to Google Voice ($20 one-time fee), so you keep access to calls and texts from it via the app—but stop paying the $150 plan.
  • Use the business line full-time for calls/data
  • That way, you don’t lose legacy access or relationships, but save money and consolidate your mobile identity moving forward
