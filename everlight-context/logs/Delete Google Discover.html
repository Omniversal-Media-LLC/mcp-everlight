That screen is coming from Google Discover / Google News Feed, which is built into most Android phones (especially if you swipe right from your home screen or open the Google app).

Unfortunately, it’s not a normal “app” you can just uninstall — but you can disable or hide it so it never pops up again. Here’s how:

Option 1 – Turn Off Google Discover in Home Screen Settings

  1. Go to your Home Screen.
  2. Long press on an empty space until you see the home screen edit mode.
  3. Swipe to the leftmost page (where this briefing/news feed lives).
  4. Look for a “Google Discover” toggle and turn it OFF.

Option 2 – Disable Discover in the Google App

  1. Open the Google app.
  2. Tap your profile picture (top right).
  3. Go to Settings → General.
  4. Find Discover and toggle it OFF.

Option 3 – Disable or Remove the Google App Entirely

Only if you don’t need Google’s voice search or feed at all. 1. Go to Settings → Apps → Google. 2. Tap Disable (you might have to uninstall updates first). 3. Confirm.

Option 4 – Switch to a Different Launcher

If your phone forces the feed, install another launcher like Nova Launcher or Microsoft Launcher from the Play Store. They don’t show the Google feed unless you want it.
