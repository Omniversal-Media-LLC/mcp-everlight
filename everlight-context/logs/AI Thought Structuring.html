What AI writing tends to do—and what you're observing—is:

  • Give a quick real-world anchor or motivation (“something observable predicted…”).
  • Introduce a core concept concisely (“here’s my central thesis: resonance”).
  • Lay out promise or structure (“here’s what it will connect: quantum, gravity, consciousness…”).

That scaffolding is unbelievably helpful for our brains. Instead of drowning in jargon or math from sentence one, you're eased into the idea.

  • Beg with why it matters (prediction aligned with observation),
  • Then what it is (a unifying theory),
