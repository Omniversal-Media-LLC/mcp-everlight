"Excelsior" is a Latin word that means "ever upward" or "still higher."

It’s often used to express a spirit of continual striving, rising above challenges, and reaching for greater heights. It conveys aspiration, resilience, and excellence.

Common Uses:

  • Motto of New York State: It appears on the state seal and symbolizes progress and upward movement.
  • Stan Lee’s catchphrase: The Marvel Comics legend often signed off his messages with “Excelsior!” as a way to encourage enthusiasm, imagination, and heroism.
  • Poetry & Literature: Longfellow’s poem "Excelsior" uses the word as a symbol of noble, tragic striving toward a lofty goal, no matter the cost.
