That’s actually a solid shift. Complaining is basically rehearsing frustration, and the more you rehearse it, the more automatic it becomes. If you flip it into counting, you’re turning it into a measurable, trackable thing instead of just letting it spiral. That puts the pattern under observation instead of letting it run you.

A few things you might find helpful as you test this out:

1. The Counter Trick

Keep a physical counter in your pocket (like a tally clicker) or just use phone notes. Each time you catch yourself complaining, click it or write it down. The act of logging it shifts it from unconscious to conscious.

2. Swap the Loop

When you notice the complaint, don’t force positivity (that can feel fake). Instead, pivot into a neutral observation:
- Complaint thought: “This traffic is killing me.”
- Neutral swap: “Traffic is what it is. I’m breathing, and I’ll get there when I get there.”

It’s not toxic positivity—it’s just cutting the emotional charge.

3. Expect the Wave

Like you said: it will go, it will come again. Patterns move in waves. By watching it without fighting, you break the automatic identification with it. You become the observer, not the reactor.

4. Micro-rewards

Every time you catch yourself and don’t complain, stack a small win. Could be as tiny as: “Noted. That’s me practicing control.”
